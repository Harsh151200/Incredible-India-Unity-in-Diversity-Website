Food And Cusines

Kanda Phoha


Kanda Poha is very tasty and quick filling breakfast. Kanda Poha is a dish from Maharashtrian cuisine. Kanda Poha is a snack made from flattened rice. It is most likely served with tea and is probably the most likely dish that a Maharashtrian will offer to his guest. During arranged marriages in Maharashtra, Kanda Poha (literal translation, poha prepared with onion) is most likely the dish served when the two families meet.

Ragi Muddle


It is a staple food of in some parts of Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh. Ragi is a finger millet and mudde is a moist ball. It is nutritious, healthy and whole some food of some daily workers. Ragi is also known as poor man’s grain from decades as they are easy to grow from minimal amount of water.

Ambot-tik


Ambot is the Konkani word for Sour and Tik means Spicy. Goan Prawn Curry with Coconut is a spicy, sour curry that comes from Goa and is also called Ambot Tik. Just serve this with steamed rice and you have a truly satisfying meal.

Alsi ki Pinni


It is a sweet dish made in northern India during winter season. These pinnis are full of nutrients and super tasty. During winters it keeps the persons body warm and it is a traditional dish as well.

Palada Paysam


Payasam is prepared with palada which is a common sweet delicacy prepared in almost all Kerala house during Onam and many more other occasions. It uses basic ingredients like rice ada, milk, sugar and ghee.

Seekh kebabs


Seekh kebab is a type of kebab, popular in South Asia, made with spiced minced or ground meat, usually lamb, beef, or chicken, formed into cylinders on skewers and grilled. It is typically cooked on a mangal or barbecue, or in a tandoor.

Lassi


Lassi is a popular traditional dahi-based drink that originated in India. Lassi is a blend of yoghurt, water, spices and sometimes fruit. Namkeen lassi is similar to doogh, while sweet and mango lassis are like milkshakes.

Gatte ki sabzi


An traditional dish from the state of Rajasthan – chickpea flour dumplings are boiled and then simmered in a yogurt based tangy curry. Gatte ki sabzi tastes great with roti or plain rice! A great alternative for those days when you have no veggies in your refrigerator. Gatte ki sabzi is really good with roti or rice.

Mawa kachoris


Mawa Kachoris are Indian hand pies filled with mawa/khoya (evaporated milk solids) and mixed nuts, deep fried and dipped in sugar syrup. These Kachoris or pies are a specialty from the State of Rajasthan.

Hara Dhania cholia


Hara Dhania Choliya is an authentic dish of Haryana. Choliya or green channa is mixed with other vegetables like onion, carrots, and spices. The dish is a Sabzi and is had along with rice or roti.

Bebinca


Bebinca, also known as bibik or bebinka, is a type of pudding and a traditional Indo-Portuguese dessert. Traditional Bebinca has seven layers. The ingredients include plain flour, sugar, ghee, egg yolk, and coconut milk. It is a traditional sweet of Goa, specially popular during Christmas.
